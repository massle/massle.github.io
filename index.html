Marcel Steinmetz
Post-Doctoral Researcher
Marcel Steinmetz is a postdoc at LAAS-CNRS and ANITI, University of Toulouse. Prior to that, he did a postdoc in the Foundations of Artificial Intelligence group headed by Jörg Hoffmann at Saarland University, where he also obtained his Ph.D.
He has been working on various aspects gravitating around AI planning, amongst others, conflict-driven learning techniques in AI planning akin to clause learning in SAT; MDP probabilistic planning and quantiative model checking; planning with description logics; explainable AI planning; simulated network penetration testing and web-security analysis via AI planning methods; and most recently on testing and verifying machine-learned policies.

Software

PFD (Probabilistic Fast Downward)
cpp
python

Extension of the Fast Downward planning system by the support of PPDDL encoded MDPs. Features a variety of heuristic search engines (like LAO*, LRTDP, FRET, i-dual) and a variety of heuristics (e.g., based on all-outcomes determinization and the large space of classical planning heuristics that readily ship with Fast Downward; probabilistic pattern databases; and probabilistic occupation-measure heuristics).

Thesis version JAIR16 version

CDL-FD (Conflict-Driven Learning Fast Downward)
cpp
python

Extension of the Fast Downward planning system by conflict-driven learning methods akin to conflict-driven clause learning in SAT. In a nutshell, in general state-space search, conflicts become dead ends, i.e., states without any solution path. CDL-FD ships variants of all common heuristic search algorithm (A*, GBFS) and heuristic-guided depth-first searches that identify such conflicts while search is running; refining a dead-end detector upon each such identification. Multiple dead-end detectors are supported (based on critical-path heuristics; dead-end traps; potential heuristics; and the state-equation heuristic).

Thesis version

ASNet Policy Testing
cpp

The Neural-FD based framework for testing ASNet policies.

ICAPS22 version

PPDDL Benchmark Suite
pddl

Comprehensive set of PPDDL benchmarks composed of benchmarks from the IPPCs, Canadian travaler adaptations of resource-constrained classical planning benchmarks (NoMystery, Rovers, and TPP), and an MDP encoding of simulated network penetration testing. Each domain comes with a limited-budget as well as an unlimited-budget version. The suite consists of a suite of acyclic benchmarks (where the state space is acyclic, allowing particular algorithm enhancements) and cyclic benchmarks.
